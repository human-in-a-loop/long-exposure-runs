# PhytoGraph — cycles 33-35

## Abstract

Cycles 33-35 closed the PhytoGraph free-tier recovery sequence at the master-ledger level. Cycle 33 produced the final six-track free-tier closure synthesis, a canonical status table, and a summary figure. The auditor validated that package and confirmed that no track met the predicate required for promotion into the master `prediction_ledger.tsv` or `speculation_ledger.tsv`.

Cycles 34-35 did not add substantive scientific work in the available record. The supplied audit report treats them as terminal no-op closure cycles: no worker build, no new artifact creation, no same-axis free-tier rerun, no promise-ledger append, and no master-ledger promotion occurred. The result is therefore a validated non-promotion state, not a new biological discovery.

The final track statuses are: Track 1 `sidecar_readiness_uncontrolled`, Track 2 `H2_remains_not_supported_or_data_limited`, Track 3 `confound_limited`, Track 4 `still_data_limited`, Track 5 `H5_remains_source_biased`, and Track 6 `environment_limited_untested`.

## Introduction

PhytoGraph is a six-track plant-biology hypergraph campaign. Its original success criterion was not simply to build a dataset or website, but to produce falsifiable predictions and validate or falsify them cleanly. The master prediction ledger is therefore the controlling boundary: a track-local candidate, diagnostic, or prior does not become a campaign-level prediction unless it satisfies the recorded validation and promotion contract.

The cycles reported here follow earlier Barrier 4 and reopen work. By cycle 33, the campaign had already produced a large accepted-key substrate, track-local instruments, an Atlas surface, validation tables, ablations, and several closure reports. The remaining task was to reconcile the strongest free-tier outcome for each track without over-promoting branch-local hypotheses.

## Discussion

Cycles 33-35 did not create a new discovery. They converted the prior branch-level closure work into a final, auditor-validated non-promotion state.

That distinction matters. PhytoGraph built substantial infrastructure: a typed hypergraph substrate, track-local instruments, Atlas integration, validation and ablation packages, and formal diagnostics. But the campaign's original success criterion was stricter than infrastructure completion. A track had to produce a falsifiable prediction and clear its validation protocol. By the end of these cycles, no track had cleared that standard at the master-ledger level.

The final synthesis also prevents repeated null work. Each track now has a specific reopen predicate. Future work should vary the missing evidence axis rather than rerunning the same free-tier branch inputs.

## Open Questions

The remaining questions are framed as reopen predicates rather than open-ended research invitations:

- Can Track 1 map GBIF accepted-key sidecar evidence into the frozen WFO accepted-key namespace, or admit an audited sidecar namespace with source-density controls?
- Can Track 2 obtain accepted-key modern-failure evidence and multi-source/source-class support for held-out anachronism cases?
- Can Track 3 obtain broader trait coverage and controls that separate convergence from family size, sampling density, projection loss, and source dominance?
- Can Track 4 generate audited crop/CWR BIOCLIM summaries plus disjoint candidate-level expert comparator rows?
- Can Track 5 obtain enough dated, non-Duke taxon-compound rows to estimate chemistry signatures without source collapse?
- Can Track 6 run approved local/open model weights and produce audited deterministic response rows?
