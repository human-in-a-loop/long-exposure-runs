# Random Hyperbolic Surface Spectral Rigidity — cycles 40-42

## Abstract

Cycles 40-42 moved the campaign from direct consequences of Kim--Tao's theorems into a scoped finite-model benchmark program. Cycle 40 completed one last Theorem 2 proof-mining step: it extracted a fixed-cutoff pre-Sobolev local `L^2` mass estimate from the proof of eigenfunction delocalization. Cycles 41 and 42 then pivoted to the two-generator Schreier/random-permutation model as a controlled benchmark for trace-expansion mechanisms. Cycle 41 established a fixed-`k` trace-moment theorem template and reproducible variance evidence. Cycle 42 upgraded that variance evidence into a paired-word covariance mechanism, validating the next theorem target

$$
\operatorname{Var}\left(n^{-1}\operatorname{Tr}(A_n^k)\right)=O_k(n^{-2})
$$

for the Schreier benchmark, subject to a remaining all-fixed-`k` quotient-template lemma.

All three cycles were validated. M29 was accepted without repair. M30 and M31 were validated after moderate repairs to their analyzers and tests. No cycle claimed a new hyperbolic random-cover theorem beyond the scoped Kim--Tao consequences and Schreier benchmark statements.

## Introduction

The broader campaign studies Kim and Tao's paper `2603.01127`, "Eigenvalue rigidity of hyperbolic surfaces in the random cover model." Earlier cycles reconstructed the paper, built proof ledgers and computational probes, and explored several extension routes. By the end of cycle 39, the direct theorem-consequence branches had produced:

- rigidity-scale multiplicity and cluster envelopes from Theorem 1;
- `L^p`, small-set mass, and effective-support corollaries from Theorem 2.

The cycle 39 audit recommended avoiding another direct interpolation or bookkeeping cycle unless the proof of Theorem 2 contained a genuinely sharper local-mass statement. Cycle 40 therefore mined the proof architecture of Theorem 2. After that route produced a scoped fixed-cutoff statement, cycles 41-42 shifted to a separate benchmark: random Schreier graphs generated by two independent random permutations. The purpose of this model is not to prove Kim--Tao surface results, but to provide a fully inspectable trace-expansion analogue where tree subtraction, fixed-point statistics, and variance mechanisms can be tested exactly or numerically.

## Approach

The report follows the chronological arc of the three cycles.

Cycle 40, M29, inspected the proof of Theorem 2 upstream of the final `L^\infty` bound. The central question was whether the pre-trace formula and fourth-moment estimate already imply a standalone local `L^2` mass estimate before the final Sobolev or elliptic conversion.

$$
A_n=P_a+P_a^{-1}+P_b+P_b^{-1},
$$

where `P_a` and `P_b` are independent uniform permutations in `S_n`. It expanded traces into fixed points of words and compared normalized trace moments with the `4`-regular tree moments.

Cycle 42, M31, expanded the M30 variance into paired fixed-word covariances. It used labelled constraint templates to test whether all fixed paired-word covariance terms remain `O_k(1)` before the outer `n^{-2}` normalization.

## Finding 1: M29 Extracted a Fixed-Cutoff Local Mass Corollary from Theorem 2

M29 found that Theorem 2 contains a proof-level local `L^2` mass estimate before the final `L^\infty` conversion. The extracted statement is a fixed-cutoff result, not a uniform geometric ball theorem.

The controlled object is the centered pre-trace local spectral mass, raised to a fourth-power statistic `V_n`, with a primitive-power diagonal term `S` subtracted before Proposition 4.1 and Proposition 4.2 are applied. On the Theorem 2 high-probability event, for the fixed nonnegative cutoff `a in C_c^\infty(H)` used in the proof, every fiber `i` and every normalized eigenfunction below energy `Lambda0` satisfies the schematic estimate

$$
\int_{\mathbb H} a(z)\,\lvert u_j^\rho(z,i)\rvert^2\,d\operatorname{Vol}(z)
\le C\Lambda_0 n^{-\alpha_0},
\qquad
\alpha_0=\frac{1}{16(2\kappa+11)}.
$$

The M29 classification table records this as `standalone_smoothed_kernel_mass_corollary` with branch decision `advance_pretrace_local_mass_branch`.

M29 compared this statement with the M28 deterministic mass bound obtained from the final sup-norm theorem. M28's final bound gives, for a fixed-volume patch in schematic form,

$$
\int_A |u|^2 \le C\Lambda_0^3 n^{-\alpha_0}\operatorname{vol}(A).
$$

The pre-Sobolev estimate has `Lambda0` power `1` rather than `3`. At fixed energy this is a theorem-strength distinction in the energy dependence rather than an improved `n` exponent. If `Lambda0=n^b`, the improvement is `n^{2b}` in the bookkeeping model.

![M29 comparison of M28 final sup-norm-derived mass bounds, the extracted pre-trace fixed-cutoff estimate, and a comparison-only no-Sobolev-loss reference.](reports/figures/m29_local_mass_exponent_comparison.png)

![M29 proof-pipeline figure locating the fixed-cutoff local mass estimate before the final Sobolev/elliptic conversion.](reports/figures/m29_theorem2_proof_pipeline.png)

The scope boundary is central. The proof fixes a smooth cutoff `a`; it does not union over all spatial centers, dominate arbitrary nonsmooth ball indicators, or imply lower mass. The auditor explicitly validated that M29 does not claim quantum ergodicity, random-wave behavior, lower mass on balls, shrinking-ball control, nodal information, or equidistribution.

## Finding 2: M30 Turned the Schreier Probe into a Trace-Moment Benchmark

M30 shifted the campaign away from direct Kim--Tao theorem corollaries and into a finite Schreier/random-permutation benchmark. The model uses the adjacency operator

$$
A_n=P_a+P_a^{-1}+P_b+P_b^{-1}
$$

on `n` vertices. For fixed `k`, the trace expands as

$$
\operatorname{Tr}(A_n^k)
=
\sum_{w\in\{a,a^{-1},b,b^{-1}\}^k}
\operatorname{Fix}(w(P_a,P_b)).
$$

Freely reducible words act as the identity and supply the `4`-regular tree moment. Nontrivial reduced words have `O_k(1)` expected fixed points, so the normalized expectation has the theorem-template form

$$
\mathbb E[n^{-1}\operatorname{Tr}(A_n^k)]
=
m_k+O_k(n^{-1}).
$$

The tree moments were regenerated exactly through `k=10`:

| k | Tree moment |
|---:|---:|
| 0 | 1 |
| 2 | 4 |
| 4 | 28 |
| 6 | 232 |
| 8 | 2092 |
| 10 | 19864 |

Odd tree moments vanish in the generated table. Dynamic programming and free-reduction enumeration agreed.

M30 also produced reproducible simulation evidence using `n=80,140,220,320`, `24` trials, and seed `20260516`, for `960` trial rows. The centered variance slopes for the main even moments were:

| k | Fitted variance slope |
|---:|---:|
| 2 | `-1.765` |
| 4 | `-1.716` |
| 6 | `-1.762` |

M30 classified the fixed-`k` expectation as a theorem template and the variance scaling as numerical evidence. The branch decision was `advance_schreier_benchmark_program`.

The model has a free-group random permutation law and adjacency spectrum, not a surface-group cover law or hyperbolic Laplacian.

## Finding 3: M31 Validated the Schreier Variance Mechanism as the Next Theorem Target

M31 upgraded M30's empirical variance evidence into a paired-word covariance mechanism. The starting identity is

$$
\operatorname{Var}\left(n^{-1}\sum_w \operatorname{Fix}(w)\right)
=
n^{-2}\sum_{w_1,w_2}
\operatorname{Cov}\left(\operatorname{Fix}(w_1),\operatorname{Fix}(w_2)\right).
$$

The key theorem template is: if every fixed nontrivial paired-word covariance is `O_k(1)`, then

$$
\operatorname{Var}\left(n^{-1}\operatorname{Tr}(A_n^k)\right)
=
O_k(n^{-2}).
$$

M31 checked reduced word-pair templates for `k=2,4,6`. After audit repair, class-level order claims were computed from all reduced pair templates in each checked class, not from one representative. The repaired summary is:

| k | Word pairs | Reduced word types | Pair classes | Max covariance order before normalization | Normalized order |
|---:|---:|---:|---:|---|---|
| 2 | 256 | 13 | 7 | `O(1)` | `O(n^-2)` |
| 4 | 65,536 | 121 | 8 | `O(1)` | `O(n^-2)` |
| 6 | 16,777,216 | 1,093 | 8 | `O(1)` | `O(n^-2)` |

No checked class had a positive-power covariance obstruction. Identity-containing classes were treated separately as deterministic covariance-zero terms. The detected relation classes include same reduced words, inverse reduced words, cyclic or inverse-conjugate pairs, shared powers, and generic pairs.

![M31 pair classes versus covariance-order exponents for `k=2,4,6`.](reports/figures/m31_pair_class_covariance_orders.png)

![M31 mechanism map from paired trace expansion to labelled-template order certification.](reports/figures/m31_variance_order_mechanism_map.png)

M31 also reinterpreted the M30 slopes near `-1.7`. Since the pair-template evidence found no `O(n)` covariance mechanism in checked classes, the validated interpretation is finite-size crossover toward an `n^{-2}` asymptotic, not evidence for a genuine `n^{-1}` covariance law.

![M31 reinterpretation of M30 fitted slopes against `n^{-1}`, `n^{-2}`, and mixed finite-size models.](reports/figures/m31_m30_slope_reinterpretation.png)

The remaining theorem gap is explicit:

$$
\text{For every fixed nontrivial reduced pair }(u,v),
\text{ every consistent quotient template contributing to }
\mathbb E\operatorname{Fix}(u)\operatorname{Fix}(v)
\text{ has }
|V|-\sum_\ell |C_\ell|\le 0.
$$

A proof of this finite quotient-template exponent lemma would turn the M31 theorem template into a fixed-`k` Schreier variance theorem. M31 does not transfer this conclusion to Kim--Tao random covers; doing so would require a new surface-group quotient-family theorem.

## Discussion

The three-cycle sequence has two parts.

First, M29 closes the last direct Theorem 2 proof-consequence branch. It shows that the proof contains a sharper fixed-cutoff local mass statement than the final sup-norm corollary alone reveals. This is a genuine theorem-level extraction, but it remains tied to the proof's chosen smooth cutoff and does not become a spatial equidistribution theorem.

Second, M30-M31 establish the Schreier benchmark as the current productive branch. M30 supplies the fixed-`k` trace-moment baseline and reproducible variance evidence. M31 explains that evidence through paired fixed-word covariance templates and identifies the exact finite combinatorial lemma still needed for a theorem.

The analogy with Kim--Tao is structural. In both settings, one expands a trace or two-trace statistic, separates deterministic or diagonal contributions, and reduces random fluctuation control to fixed-point or quotient-template counting. The analogy stops before any hyperbolic conclusion: the Schreier model has independent free generators, while Kim--Tao's random covers involve surface-group relations, Selberg or pre-trace formula inputs, and external quotient-family machinery.

## Open Questions

The immediate next question is the M31 fixed-pair exponent lemma. Proving it for arbitrary fixed nontrivial reduced word pairs would complete the finite Schreier variance theorem target.

A second route is to use M30-M31 as the validated benchmark base for finite non-shrinking spectral statistics. That route would remain finite-model work unless a separate surface-group bridge is developed.

The M29 local-mass result also leaves a proof-theoretic question: whether the fixed smooth cutoff can be replaced by a controlled finite family of geometric patches. The current cycles do not establish that extension.

The campaign should still avoid the unsupported claims excluded by the audits: quantum ergodicity, random-wave behavior, lower mass on arbitrary balls, shrinking-ball statistics, nodal information, simplicity, level repulsion, or hyperbolic local statistics from M27-M31 alone.
