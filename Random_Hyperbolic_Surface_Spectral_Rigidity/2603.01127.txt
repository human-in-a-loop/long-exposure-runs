                                                  Abstract. Let X be a compact connected orientable hyperbolic surface and let Xn be
                                                  a degree n random cover. We show that, with high probability, the distribution of eigen-
                                                  values of the Laplacian on Xn converges to the spectral measure of the hyperbolic plane
                                                  with polynomially decaying error. This is analogous to the eigenvalue rigidity property for
                                                  graphs [HY24] and improves the logarithmic bound of [Mon22]. We also obtain a polyno-
                                                  mial improvement on the L8 bound of the eigenfunctions. Our proof relies on the Selberg
                                                  trace formula and a variant of the polynomial method.

1.1. Previous work. We briefly review prior results on the low energy spectral theory
(i.e., the restriction to bounded eigenvalues) of random hyperbolic surfaces. Most of such
work focused on the spectral gap. The spectral gap appears when studying the error term
for counting closed geodesics of bounded length, the error term for the hyperbolic lattice
counting problem, and the error term for the rate of mixing of the geodesic flow. The
spectral gap on hyperbolic surfaces can be thought of as an analog of the AlonвҖ“Boppana
bound on graphs. For additional context on spectral gaps, see the survey [MN26].
