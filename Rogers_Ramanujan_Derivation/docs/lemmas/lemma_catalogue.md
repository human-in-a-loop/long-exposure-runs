# Lemma Catalogue: Rogers-Ramanujan Foundation

Status labels: `proved`, `conjectured`, `experimentally_verified`, `rejected`.

## Definitions

Let `R[[q]]` denote the ring of formal power series over a commutative ring `R`.
Coefficient extraction is written `[q^k]A(q)`. For `K >= 0`,

\[
A(q) \equiv B(q) \pmod {q^{K+1}}
\]

means `[q^k]A=[q^k]B` for every `0 <= k <= K`.

The finite q-Pochhammer symbol used here is

\[
(q;q)_n=\prod_{j=1}^n(1-q^j),\qquad (q;q)_0=1.
\]

Each `(q;q)_n` has constant term `1`, so it is invertible in `Z[[q]]`.

## L0. Coefficientwise Equality

**Status:** `proved`

Two formal series `A,B in Z[[q]]` are equal if and only if `[q^k]A=[q^k]B` for every `k >= 0`.
This is the definition of equality in the product set `Z^N` underlying `Z[[q]]`.

## L1. Coefficientwise Stabilization and Limits

**Status:** `proved`

Let `(A_N)_{N>=0}` be a sequence in `Z[[q]]`. If for every fixed `k` the integer
`[q^k]A_N` is eventually constant, define

\[
[q^k]A=\lim_{N\to\infty}[q^k]A_N
\]

as that eventual value. Then `A in Z[[q]]` is the unique coefficientwise limit of `A_N`.

Proof: every coefficient is independently an eventually constant integer, hence determines exactly one element of `Z^N`.
Uniqueness follows from L0.

## L2. Truncation Transfer Lemma

**Status:** `proved`

Suppose `A_N -> A` and `B_N -> B` coefficientwise in the eventual-stabilization sense of L1.
If for every `K >= 0` there exists `N_0(K)` such that

\[
A_N(q)\equiv B_N(q)\pmod {q^{K+1}}\qquad\text{for all }N\ge N_0(K),
\]

then `A=B` in `Z[[q]]`.

Proof: fix `k`. Choose `K=k`. For all large `N`, `[q^k]A_N=[q^k]B_N`.
By coefficientwise stabilization, the left side is eventually `[q^k]A` and the right side is eventually `[q^k]B`.
Thus `[q^k]A=[q^k]B` for every `k`; apply L0.

## L3. Safe Truncation of the Rogers-Ramanujan Series Sides

**Status:** `proved`

Modulo `q^{K+1}`,

\[
\sum_{n\ge0}\frac{q^{n^2}}{(q;q)_n}
\equiv
\sum_{0\le n\le \lfloor\sqrt K\rfloor}\frac{q^{n^2}}{(q;q)_n},
\]

and

\[
\sum_{n\ge0}\frac{q^{n^2+n}}{(q;q)_n}
\equiv
\sum_{n\ge0,\ n^2+n\le K}\frac{q^{n^2+n}}{(q;q)_n}.
\]

Proof: `(q;q)_n^{-1}` has lowest degree `0`. Therefore the lowest degree in the `n`th summand is `n^2` in the first series and `n^2+n` in the second.
Terms whose lowest degree exceeds `K` cannot affect coefficients through degree `K`.

## L4. Safe Truncation of Residue-Class Products

**Status:** `proved`

For fixed `K`, the coefficient through degree `K` of

\[
\prod_{\substack{j\ge1\\j\bmod 5\in R}}(1-q^j)^{-1}
\]

depends only on factors with `j <= K`.

Proof: every omitted factor has the form `1 + q^j + q^{2j} + ...` with `j>K`, so its nonconstant terms have degree greater than `K`.
The omitted infinite tail is therefore congruent to `1` modulo `q^{K+1}`.

## L5. Gap-Two Staircase Interpretation, Smallest Part at Least 1

**Status:** `proved`

The summand

\[
\frac{q^{n^2}}{(q;q)_n}
\]

generates partitions into exactly `n` positive parts with adjacent gaps at least `2`.

Proof: write such a partition in weakly decreasing order
`\lambda_1>\lambda_2>\cdots>\lambda_n>=1` with `\lambda_i-\lambda_{i+1}>=2`.
Subtract the minimal gap-two staircase

\[
(2n-1,2n-3,\ldots,3,1),
\]

whose weight is `n^2`. The residual sequence

\[
\mu_i=\lambda_i-(2(n-i)+1)
\]

is weakly decreasing and nonnegative, hence is an ordinary partition with at most `n` parts.
Conversely, adding the staircase to any weakly decreasing nonnegative `\mu_1,\ldots,\mu_n` gives a positive gap-two partition with `n` parts.
The generating function for weakly decreasing nonnegative `n`-tuples is `(q;q)_n^{-1}`, so the claimed summand follows after multiplying by the staircase weight `q^{n^2}`.

## L6. Gap-Two Staircase Interpretation, Smallest Part at Least 2

**Status:** `proved`

The summand

\[
\frac{q^{n^2+n}}{(q;q)_n}
\]

generates partitions into exactly `n` parts with adjacent gaps at least `2` and smallest part at least `2`.

Proof: subtract

\[
(2n,2n-2,\ldots,4,2),
\]

whose weight is `n^2+n`. The same argument as L5 gives a bijection with weakly decreasing nonnegative `n`-tuples, generated by `(q;q)_n^{-1}`.

## L7. Product Coefficients for Allowed Residues

**Status:** `proved`

For `R subset {0,1,2,3,4}`, the product

\[
\prod_{\substack{j\ge1\\j\bmod 5\in R}}(1-q^j)^{-1}
\]

generates partitions whose parts all have residues in `R` modulo `5`.

Proof: each factor expands as `1+q^j+q^{2j}+...`, choosing the multiplicity of part `j`.
Multiplying finitely modulo `q^{K+1}` is justified by L4; coefficientwise passage to the full product is justified by L1.

## L8. Auxiliary Q-Difference Identity

**Status:** `proved`

For

\[
F(z,q)=\sum_{n\ge0}\frac{z^nq^{n^2}}{(q;q)_n},
\]

the formal identity

\[
F(z,q)-F(zq,q)=zqF(zq^2,q)
\]

holds in the coefficientwise sense. Proof is by subtracting terms, canceling `(1-q^n)/(q;q)_n`, and reindexing; see `docs/proof/q_difference_mechanism.md`.

## L9. Q-Difference Ladder

**Status:** `proved`

With

\[
A_r(q)=F(q^r,q),
\]

the sequence satisfies

\[
A_r=A_{r+1}+q^{r+1}A_{r+2},\qquad r\ge0.
\]

Here `A_0` and `A_1` are the two Rogers-Ramanujan series sides.

## L10. Tail-Normalized Ladder Uniqueness

**Status:** `proved`

The recurrence in L9 has at most one solution sequence satisfying `C_r -> 1` coefficientwise as `r -> infinity`. Existence is supplied by the explicit `A_r` series. Thus the series side is the unique tail-normalized solution of the infinite ladder.

## L11. Finite Largest-Part Gap Recurrence

**Status:** `proved`

Let `G_N^(a)` generate gap-two partitions with all parts at least `a` and largest part at most `N`, with the empty partition allowed. Then `G_N^(a)=1` for `N<a` and

\[
G_N^{(a)}=G_{N-1}^{(a)}+q^NG_{N-2}^{(a)}\qquad(N\ge a).
\]

Proof is by splitting on whether the largest allowed part `N` is present; see `docs/proof/finite_gap_recurrence.md`.

## L12. Euler Complement Product Reduction

**Status:** `proved`

Multiplying the two target Rogers-Ramanujan identities by
`E(q)=(q;q)_infinity` is formally equivalent to proving the complementary
products

\[
E(q)S_1=(q^2;q^5)_\infty(q^3;q^5)_\infty(q^5;q^5)_\infty,
\]

and

\[
E(q)S_2=(q;q^5)_\infty(q^4;q^5)_\infty(q^5;q^5)_\infty.
\]

Proof: split `E(q)` by residue classes modulo 5 and cancel unit factors
with constant term `1`; see `docs/proof/product_transform_route.md`.

## L13. Finite q-Binomial Theorem

**Status:** `proved`

For `N>=0`,

\[
(z;Q)_N=\sum_{k=0}^{N}(-1)^kQ^{k(k-1)/2}{N\brack k}_Qz^k.
\]

Proof is by induction on `N` using Gaussian Pascal recurrence. The
recurrence also proves the Gaussian coefficients are polynomials.

## L14. Derived Jacobi-Type Product Identity

**Status:** `proved`

The finite identity

\[
(z;Q)_N(Q/z;Q)_N
=\sum_{j=-N}^{N}(-1)^jz^jQ^{j(j-1)/2}{2N\brack N+j}_Q
\]

follows from L13 by convolution. Passing coefficientwise to the limit gives

\[
(z;Q)_\infty(Q/z;Q)_\infty(Q;Q)_\infty
=\sum_{j\in\mathbb Z}(-1)^jz^jQ^{j(j-1)/2}.
\]

Specializing `(Q,z)=(q^5,q^2)` gives exponents `j(5j-1)/2`;
specializing `(Q,z)=(q^5,q)` gives exponents `j(5j-3)/2`.

## L15. Euler-Multiplied Finite Series Recurrence

**Status:** `proved`

Let

\[
H_{\alpha,N}=\sum_{n=0}^{N}q^{n^2+\alpha n}\frac{(q;q)_N}{(q;q)_n}.
\]

Then

\[
H_{\alpha,N}=(1-q^N)H_{\alpha,N-1}+q^{N^2+\alpha N},\qquad H_{\alpha,0}=1.
\]

Proof: split off the `n=N` term and factor
`(q;q)_N=(1-q^N)(q;q)_(N-1)` from all earlier terms.

## L16. Transformed-Series Bilateral Collapse

**Status:** `conjectured`

Experiments support

\[
\lim_{N\to\infty}H_{0,N}=\sum_{j\in\mathbb Z}(-1)^jq^{j(5j-1)/2},
\qquad
\lim_{N\to\infty}H_{1,N}=\sum_{j\in\mathbb Z}(-1)^jq^{j(5j-3)/2},
\]

but this cycle did not prove the collapse. The natural finite comparison
matches only through degree `N`, and simple Schur-style Gaussian windows
tested in `scripts/rr/product_transform_probe.wls` fail at degree `1` for
`N=24`.

## L17. Finite Signed-Object Expansion For Transformed Sums

**Status:** `proved`

For \(\alpha\in\{0,1\}\),

\[
H_{\alpha,N}
=\sum_{n=0}^N\sum_{S\subseteq\{n+1,\ldots,N\}}
(-1)^{|S|}q^{n^2+\alpha n+\sum_{s\in S}s}.
\]

Proof: expand
`(q;q)_N/(q;q)_n=Product[1-q^s,{s,n+1,N}]` by choosing either `1` or
`-q^s` from each factor. See `docs/proof/transformed_series_collapse.md`.

## L18. Local Boundary Absorb/Release Involution

**Status:** `rejected`

The tested local rule moves the boundary `n` by absorbing selected tail
parts at or below the new boundary, or by releasing missing parts between
the old and new boundary, subject to the necessary weight equations

\[
r(2n+\alpha+r)
\quad\text{and}\quad
r(2n+\alpha-r).
\]

The accepted moves are weight-preserving, sign-reversing, and involutive,
but the rule is not total. At `KMAX=40, NMAX=12`, it leaves `1887/4059`
objects unpaired for `alpha=0` and all `3865` tested objects unpaired for
`alpha=1`; see `scripts/rr/transformed_cancellation_probe.wls`.

## L19. Finite Transformed-Series Collapse

**Status:** `conjectured`

The desired finite boundary statement is that `H_(alpha,N)` agrees with
the corresponding bilateral pentagonal series through a growing initial
coefficient range, with boundary corrections escaping to high degree.
The signed-object probe supports this pattern, but no finite exact
collapse or complete cancellation rule was proved in this cycle.

## L20. Finite Recurrence/State System For Transformed Sums

**Status:** `proved` for scalar recurrence; `conjectured` for a sufficient state system

The scalar recurrence

\[
H_{\alpha,N}=(1-q^N)H_{\alpha,N-1}+q^{N^2+\alpha N}
\]

proves coefficientwise stabilization of `H_(alpha,N)`, since
`H_(alpha,N)-H_(alpha,N-1)` has lowest possible degree at least `N`.
However, this one-state recurrence does not identify the stabilized
coefficients with the bilateral pentagonal series. A proof-bearing
fallback likely needs additional shifted or modulo-five states.

## L21. Pure Nonlocal Subset-Transfer Rule

**Status:** `rejected`

For signed objects `(n,S)` in L17, a broader candidate than L18 allows an
upward move `n -> n+r` by removing any odd subset
`A subset S` with

\[
\sum A=r(2n+\alpha+r),
\]

and a downward move `n -> n-r` by adding any odd missing subset with

\[
\sum A=r(2n+\alpha-r).
\]

These equations are necessary for weight preservation and sign reversal.
The tested deterministic smallest-`r` rules have no weight/sign/tail
failures for accepted transfers, but they are neither total nor
involutive; see `docs/proof/nonlocal_transformed_involution.md`.

## L22. Singleton-Tail Obstruction To Pure Transfer

**Status:** `proved` as an obstruction to L21

For \(\alpha=0\), the object `(0,{t})` cannot move downward and can move
upward under the pure transfer rule only when \(t=r^2\). Thus `(0,{5})`
is a stable non-pentagonal no-move object for every `N>=5`.

For \(\alpha=1\), the same argument forces \(t=r(r+1)\). Thus `(0,{3})`
is a stable non-pentagonal no-move object for every `N>=3`.

Therefore a successful transformed-series cancellation cannot be a pure
exact subset transfer between the staircase and signed tail alone.

## L23. Canonical Smallest-r Nonlocal Transfer

**Status:** `rejected`

Even where a pure transfer exists, the canonical smallest-`r` rule need
not be involutive. For example, at \(\alpha=0\), `(0,{4})` moves upward
by `r=2` to `(2,{})`, but the smallest admissible reverse move from
`(2,{})` releases `{3}` and lands at `(1,{3})`. This rejects the tested
canonical tie-breaker family independently of the singleton no-move
obstruction.

## L24. Coefficient Recurrence For H

**Status:** `proved`

Writing

\[
H_{\alpha,N}(q)=\sum_{k\ge0}h_{\alpha,N}(k)q^k,
\]

L15 gives

\[
h_{\alpha,N}(k)
=h_{\alpha,N-1}(k)-h_{\alpha,N-1}(k-N)
+\mathbf 1_{k=N^2+\alpha N},
\]

with \(h_{\alpha,N-1}(r)=0\) for `r<0`. This triangular recurrence proves
that \(h_{\alpha,N}(k)\) stabilizes once `N>k`.

## L25. Shifted Diagonal Recurrence

**Status:** `proved`

For diagonal states

\[
T_{\alpha,N}(d)=h_{\alpha,N}(N+d),
\]

L24 gives the exact recurrence

\[
T_{\alpha,N}(d)
=T_{\alpha,N-1}(d+1)-h_{\alpha,N-1}(d)
+\mathbf 1_{d=N^2+(\alpha-1)N}.
\]

This is a useful diagnostic recurrence, but it shifts the required
boundary offset from `d` to `d+1`.

## L26. Fixed Diagonal-Window Modulo-5 Closure

**Status:** `rejected` for the direct finite-window system derived from H alone

For any fixed window `0<=d<=W`, L25 requires the outside state
`T_(alpha,N-1)(W+1)` to update `T_(alpha,N)(W)`. Iterating five steps
only moves the missing boundary to `T_(alpha,N-5)(W+5)`, so splitting by
`N mod 5` does not close the system. This does not rule out richer
state systems, but it rejects the direct finite offset-window route
tested in `docs/proof/mod5_state_recurrence.md`.

## L27. Euler-Tail Double-Sum Expansion

**Status:** `proved`

For \(\alpha\in\{0,1\}\),

\[
(q;q)_\infty\sum_{n\ge0}\frac{q^{n^2+\alpha n}}{(q;q)_n}
=
\sum_{n,k\ge0}
\frac{(-1)^kq^{n^2+\alpha n+kn+k(k+1)/2}}{(q;q)_k}.
\]

Proof: use L13 with \(z=q^{n+1}\) and pass coefficientwise to
\((q^{n+1};q)_\infty\), obtaining

\[
(q^{n+1};q)_\infty
=\sum_{k\ge0}\frac{(-1)^kq^{k(k+1)/2+kn}}{(q;q)_k}.
\]

Then cancel
\((q;q)_\infty/(q;q)_n=(q^{n+1};q)_\infty\). See
`docs/proof/euler_tail_telescoping.md`.

## L28. Low-Order Euler-Tail Divergence Certificate

**Status:** `rejected` for the tested ansatz

Let \(F_\alpha(n,k)\) be the summand in L27, and put \(x=q^n,y=q^k\).
The tested certificates have
\(U=A(x,y)F_\alpha(n,k)\), \(V=B(x,y)F_\alpha(n,k)\), and require the
normalized rational identity

\[
A(x,y)-q^{\alpha+1}x^2yA(qx,y)
+B(x,y)+\frac{qxy}{1-qy}B(x,qy)=1.
\]

For polynomial \(A,B\) of total degree at most `3`, including monomial
support diagnostics by \(2n+k+\alpha\bmod 5\), the cleared polynomial
coefficient equations have no solution for \(\alpha=0\) or \(\alpha=1\).
This rejects only this low-order rational divergence class; it does not
rule out richer telescoping with piecewise residue classes, additional
denominators, coordinates, or summation parameters.

## L31. Static Beta/Abacus Signature Maps

**Status:** `rejected` for the tested signatures

The tested static signatures combine length, beta-set runner counts,
shifted beta-set runner counts, and quotient sums. They fail at low
weights because the gap and residue sides either have different
signatures or a signature is not one-to-one. Minimal examples include
\(\alpha=0\), \(\lambda=(1)\), for shifted-runner signatures, and
\(\alpha=1\), \(\lambda=(4)\), for both length-plus-runner signatures.
Thus these low-information abacus statistics do not define a canonical
weight-preserving bijection.

## L32. Independent Nearest-Runner Bead Slide

**Status:** `rejected`

The probe also tested the natural rule that slides each standard- or
shifted-beta bead independently to the nearest allowed runner modulo `5`
and treats the resulting positions as residue parts. This changes total
weight in minimal cases, e.g. for \(\alpha=0\), \(\lambda=(2)\) maps the
single beta bead from runner `2` to part `1`, changing weight from `2` to
`1`. Hence any successful abacus bijection cannot be independent nearest
runner normalization; it needs compensating global structure or a
different recursive rule.

## L33. Derived Triangular Matrix Inversion

**Status:** `proved`

For

\[
M^{(a)}_{n,r}=\frac{1}{(q;q)_{n-r}(aq;q)_{n+r}},
\qquad 0\le r\le n,
\]

the triangular relation \(\beta_n=\sum_{r=0}^nM^{(a)}_{n,r}\alpha_r\)
has inverse

\[
\alpha_n=\sum_{j=0}^n
\frac{1-aq^{2n}}{1-a}
\frac{(a;q)_{n+j}}{(q;q)_{n-j}}
(-1)^{n-j}q^{(n-j)(n-j-1)/2}\beta_j.
\]

The proof multiplies the two triangular matrices. Off-diagonal entries
reduce to finite q-binomial sums
\(\sum_k(-1)^kq^{k(k-1)/2}{m\brack k}_qq^{-tk}=0\) with \(0\le t<m\);
diagonal entries are \(1\). See `docs/proof/bailey_matrix_transform.md`.

## L34. Rogers-Ramanujan Triangular Pairs

**Status:** `proved`

For \(\beta_n=1/(q;q)_n\), the inverse in L33 gives the two explicit
alpha sequences

\[
\alpha^{(0)}_0=1,\qquad
\alpha^{(0)}_r=(-1)^rq^{r(3r-1)/2}(1+q^r)\quad(r\ge1),
\]

and

\[
\alpha^{(1)}_r=(-1)^rq^{r(3r+1)/2}\frac{1-q^{2r+1}}{1-q}
\quad(r\ge0).
\]

Equivalently,

\[
\sum_{r=0}^n
\frac{\alpha^{(0)}_r}{(q;q)_{n-r}(q;q)_{n+r}}
=\frac{1}{(q;q)_n},
\]

and

\[
\sum_{r=0}^n
\frac{\alpha^{(1)}_r}{(q;q)_{n-r}(q^2;q)_{n+r}}
=\frac{1}{(q;q)_n}.
\]

The terminating evaluations are finite q-binomial consequences of L33.
The script `scripts/rr/bailey_matrix_probe.wls` independently verifies
the solved forms by clearing denominators for \(0\le n\le12\).

## L35. Coefficientwise Limiting Matrix Transform

**Status:** `proved`

If \((\alpha,\beta)\) satisfy the triangular relation in L33, then

\[
\sum_{n\ge0}a^nq^{n^2}\beta_n
=
\frac{1}{(aq;q)_\infty}\sum_{r\ge0}a^rq^{r^2}\alpha_r.
\]

Proof: substitute the finite triangular relation and set \(n=r+s\).
The inner sum is reduced to

\[
\sum_{s\ge0}\frac{z^sq^{s(s-1)}(zq^s;q)_\infty}{(q;q)_s}=1,
\qquad z=aq^{2r+1}.
\]

Expanding \((zq^s;q)_\infty\) by L13 and collecting the coefficient of
\(z^m\) gives a multiple of \((1;q)_m\), hence zero for \(m>0\) and one
for \(m=0\). Coefficientwise interchange is justified by L1-L2.

## L36. Bailey-Matrix Transfer to Jacobi Products

**Status:** `proved`

Applying L35 to the two pairs in L34 gives

\[
\sum_{n\ge0}\frac{q^{n^2}}{(q;q)_n}
=\frac{1}{(q;q)_\infty}
\sum_{j\in\mathbb Z}(-1)^jq^{j(5j-1)/2},
\]

and

\[
\sum_{n\ge0}\frac{q^{n^2+n}}{(q;q)_n}
=\frac{1}{(q^2;q)_\infty(1-q)}
\sum_{j\in\mathbb Z}(-1)^jq^{j(5j-3)/2}.
\]

Using the Jacobi product identities already proved in L14, these are
exactly the two Rogers-Ramanujan product identities after cancellation
of the unit \((q;q)_\infty\).

## L37. Formal Product Invertibility and Cancellation

**Status:** `proved`

Every coefficientwise-defined product

\[
(q^{r_1},\ldots,q^{r_t};q^d)_\infty
=\prod_{i=1}^t\prod_{m\ge0}(1-q^{r_i+dm})
\]

with \(r_i,d\ge1\) has constant term \(1\), so it is a unit in
\(\mathbb Z[[q]]\). Therefore product cancellation in the final
Rogers-Ramanujan step is legitimate formal multiplication by inverses.
See `docs/proof/product_side.md`.

## L38. Euler Modulo-5 Factorization

**Status:** `proved`

Splitting the Euler product by the residue class of each positive integer
modulo \(5\) gives

\[
(q;q)_\infty=(q,q^2,q^3,q^4,q^5;q^5)_\infty.
\]

This is a coefficientwise regrouping: for each fixed degree, only finitely
many Euler factors can affect the coefficient.

## L39. Product-Side Closure for RR1

**Status:** `proved`

By L36 and L14,

\[
(q;q)_\infty\sum_{n\ge0}\frac{q^{n^2}}{(q;q)_n}
=(q^2,q^3,q^5;q^5)_\infty.
\]

Using L38 and cancelling the unit factors from L37 gives

\[
\sum_{n\ge0}\frac{q^{n^2}}{(q;q)_n}
=\frac{1}{(q,q^4;q^5)_\infty}
=\prod_{m\ge0}\frac{1}{(1-q^{5m+1})(1-q^{5m+4})}.
\]

## L40. Product-Side Closure for RR2

**Status:** `proved`

\[
(q;q)_\infty\sum_{n\ge0}\frac{q^{n^2+n}}{(q;q)_n}
=(q,q^4,q^5;q^5)_\infty.

Using L38 and cancelling the unit factors from L37 gives

\sum_{n\ge0}\frac{q^{n^2+n}}{(q;q)_n}
=\frac{1}{(q^2,q^3;q^5)_\infty}
=\prod_{m\ge0}\frac{1}{(1-q^{5m+2})(1-q^{5m+3})}.
\]

## L41. Rogers-Ramanujan Identity I

**Status:** `proved`

The first Rogers-Ramanujan identity holds in \(\mathbb Z[[q]]\):

\[
\sum_{n\ge0}\frac{q^{n^2}}{(q;q)_n}
=\prod_{m\ge0}\frac{1}{(1-q^{5m+1})(1-q^{5m+4})}.
\]

Proof: L13-L14 derive the needed Jacobi product identity, L33-L36 derive
the Bailey-style triangular transfer from the series side to the bilateral
Jacobi series, and L37-L39 perform the formal product cancellation. See
`docs/proof/final_proof.md`.

## L42. Rogers-Ramanujan Identity II

**Status:** `proved`

The second Rogers-Ramanujan identity holds in \(\mathbb Z[[q]]\):

\[
\sum_{n\ge0}\frac{q^{n^2+n}}{(q;q)_n}
=\prod_{m\ge0}\frac{1}{(1-q^{5m+2})(1-q^{5m+3})}.
\]

Proof: L13-L14 derive the needed Jacobi product identity, L33-L36 derive

## Candidate Mechanisms for Later Cycles

1. **Finite-polynomial recurrence route** — `experimentally_verified`: product coefficients satisfy a divisor-sum recurrence derived from logarithmic differentiation; the new largest-part recurrence is proved for the gap-two side, but a modulo-five finite product correction remains open.
2. **Coupled q-difference route** — `partially_proved`: the staircase series ladder and uniqueness are proved; product-side closure is still an open proof obligation.
3. **q-binomial/product-transform route** — `proved for M2`: Euler complement reduction, finite q-binomial theorem, Jacobi-type product identity, derived triangular matrix inversion, and the coefficientwise limiting transform now identify the transformed Rogers-Ramanujan series with the bilateral Jacobi sums.
4. **Transformed-series cancellation route** — `partially_rejected`: local boundary moves, pure nonlocal subset transfer, direct fixed diagonal-window recurrences, and low-order Euler-tail divergence certificates are rejected; any successful mechanism needs additional structure.
5. **Direct partition-bijection route** — `active`: finite gap/residue sets and simple beta/abacus diagnostics are generated; static signatures and independent nearest-runner slides are rejected as sufficient maps, but richer bijections remain possible.
